ACT 1 — THE ROUTINE

Text alternative

Too late to notice

General Description:

Act 1 Details

This short film portrays a man in his thirties who is trapped in a monotonous urban everyday life. Through repetition, visual isolation, and inner monologues, the film explores emotional numbness, alienation from other people, and the consequences of living on autopilot. A life-changing encounter with a stranger forces the man to slow down, reflect, and reconnect with the people and world around him.

Characters

The man: Dressed in a corporate coat, emotionally drained.

Act 1: The routine

A man in his thirties stands at the top of a subway staircase on a cold day in the city. It is a grey day, which emphasises the impersonal urban environment. He is wearing a business-style coat and looks physically well-groomed but emotionally drained. He is wearing black noise-cancelling headphones and looking at his smartphone. His posture is closed and withdrawn. He walks slowly and mechanically down the underground stairs.

The Choreography of the Mundane

We are all prisoners of a rhythm we did not choose. Every morning, the city breathes us in and spits us out onto the same cold platforms. The routine is a silent agreement we make with time—a comfort that slowly turns into a cage.
