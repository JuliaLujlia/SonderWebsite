ACT 1 — THE ROUTINE

The Choreography of the Mundane

We are all prisoners of a rhythm we did not choose. Every morning, the city breathes us in and spits us out onto the same cold platforms. The routine is a silent agreement we make with time—a comfort that slowly turns into a cage. We touch the same steel, walk the same tiles, and look through the same windows, yet we remain invisible to one another. In this act, the world is a loop of shadows, where the individual is lost in the mechanical heartbeat of a life lived on repeat.
